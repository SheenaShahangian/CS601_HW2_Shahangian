My Unexpected Path

My path in life has been anything but predictable. I graduated with an undergraduate degree in business and marketing with the assumption I wanted to work at a huge advertising agency. After living the ad agency life for a bit, I quickly realized it was not my type of place or lifestyle. I'm a big believer that the work you do should have meaning or make an impact, and I wasn't getting that feeling working in marketing and advertising. Every day felt the same; very tactical and never looking at the bigger picture. Something simply felt off, and I had to do something about it.

I've always been a creative soul. As a kid, I spent many an hour drawing, weaving, and immersing myself in various forms of crafts. I distinctly remembered how doing those things always brought me to life, and I began to wonder if being a creator would give me that sense of purpose and calm yet again. So in my free time in the evenings after work, I began looking into what it would look like to work as a photographer. The idea of capturing a single moment in time in a way that allowed you to re-live it fascinated me to no end, and I needed to know more.

I slowly compiled professional grade gear and took it out every weekend to practice. I dedicated as much of my time as possible immersing myself in photo courses and online learning. If I was going to do this and be in charge of capturing essential moments in people's lives, I had to be the best I possibly could. After dedicating countless hours to honing in on my craft, I launched my photo business. Needless to say, it was love at first shutter click. Things took off from there, and I quickly realized that I had found my match.

"The highest reward for a person's toil is not what they get for it, but what they become by it" - John Ruskin

Where I Find My Inspiration

When most people think about weddings, they think of huge productions. They easily forget about the true meaning behind these days; the fact that they're about connection and being inspired by another human being so much you want to commit your life to them. Elopements allow couples to get back to that meaning and intention: the true reason people get married in the first place. Elopements are extremely small ceremonies, oftentimes with only the couple themselves present. They don't incorporate all of the fuss that comes with large, traditional weddings, and it's this stripped down and minimalist approach to getting married that brings me alive and fires me up.
